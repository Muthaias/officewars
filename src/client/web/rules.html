Rules of the game

What happens in the graph stays in the graph

This is the most important rule of the game. This is a game of social manipulation and cunning, ment to played among people who know and care for each other. You are allowed, encouraged even, to trick and deceive each other within the game, and also to seek retribution within the game if and when you have been tricked or lied to. You are not, however, allowed to seek retribution in any form outside of the game. If you do, then that makes you childish and means you are not mature enough to play games with adults. If you cannot handle this, then please do not play.

How to play

Every round is 24 hours long and begins/ends at 12:50 (server local time). This means that actively playing the game a few minutes before 12:50 (the end of your lunch brake?) makes you active in every round.
The round consists of each player giving orders to their units, which will be carried out when the game ticks at the end of the round. During the round you also get to make deals with the other players, to try to get them to help you along. The social part of the game takes place outside of the game (chat, email, what have you). This means the game only works if you play with people you know and can talk to outside of the game. Each round, each of your nodes can take one of following actions:

  1. Attack another node to which it is connected.
  2. Support another node to which it is connected (either in defence or in attack). You can support other players nodes, not just your own!

The tick

At the end of each round, the game will "tick", meaning everybodys orders are carried out and a new round begins. The tick will resolve in the following way:

  1. Pinning: A node is considered pinned for the round if it is attacked. A pinned node can still attack, but cannot support. Use this to your advantage to cut support to nodes you want to take!
  2. Battle: When a node is ordered to attack another node, the strength of each node is calculated. Attackers begin at strength 1, as do defenders. There is one point of strength added for every unit that supports either the attacker or the defender respectively. If the attacker has greater strength than the defender, the node is conquered. If the defender has equal or greater strength than the attacker, nothing happens. If a node is attacked from several systems at once, only the strongest attack is considered. If there are several attackers of equal strength, then they block each other and nothing happens, even if that strength is greater than the strength of the defence.

Winning

Playing the game until only a single player remains can be tiresome (you can do that if you want). But in order to stop the game before it gets boring, there is a voting mechanism to end the game early by electing a winner. When the player with the most nodes control more than 30% more nodes than the one in second place, it becomes possible for the other players to vote to end the game and collectively surrender. Votes are wheighted by the number of nodes each player controls. If the vote carries, the player with the most nodes win the game.
